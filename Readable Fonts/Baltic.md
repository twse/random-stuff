### Fonts and keyboards
[Burti](http://www.ansis.lv/burti/index.en.php) (2003), by [Ansis Ataols Bērziņš](http://www.ansis.lv/index.en.php), is a Latvian product to type Latvian in 10 encodings but the docs are in Latvian. The author omitted the r cedilla (ŗ), a diacritic that is not used in everyday written Latvian text.

Skolar (2013), by David Březina, is an OpenType serif font designed for scholars. The font can be purchased at [Rosetta Type Foundry website](https://www.rosettatype.com/) for €20. The Pan-European version of this font contain only lowercase characters for accented Lithuanian.

### Available encodings in Burti
The `/b#` switch or `Ctrl-Number` keyboard shortcut changes the encoding whereas # is a digit.

* 1 – RST 1040-90 or RusLat DOS (default encoding), not supported in OS/2
* 2 – CP775, not supported in OS/2
* 3 – LVS 8-92/2, not supported in OS/2
* 4 – CP1257 - RIM or LVS 8-92/1 or Windows Baltic
* 5 – Baltic
* 6 – Mazuša encoding (used in macOS), not supported in OS/2
* 7 – CP1282 - Apple Central Europe, used in macOS Baltic localizations
* 8 – LR/Fortech, not supported in OS/2
* 9 – NeoFont, not supported in OS/2
* 0 – Plus, not supported in OS/2
